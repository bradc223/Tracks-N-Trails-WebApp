Track Details

Manukapua Island, also known as Big Sand Island, is a small, uninhabited island located in the Kaipara Harbour on New Zealand's North Island. It is known for its expansive sand dunes, native vegetation, and serene, untouched beauty. The island is a popular destination for those seeking a remote and peaceful escape, offering opportunities for birdwatching, beachcombing, and exploring the unique coastal environment.

Access to Manukapua Island typically requires a 4WD journey across the surrounding tidal flats, especially at low tide, when the sand is firm enough to drive on. The route can be challenging, with changing tidal conditions, soft sand, and potential boggy areas, so it's recommended for experienced drivers with suitable vehicles. Once on the island, visitors can enjoy the tranquility and natural beauty of the area, with panoramic views of the surrounding harbor and distant hills.

The island is part of a fragile ecosystem, so visitors are encouraged to respect the environment and adhere to any local guidelines to preserve its pristine condition.

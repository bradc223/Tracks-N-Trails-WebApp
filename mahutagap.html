Track Details

Mahuta Gap is a coastal area located on the west coast of New Zealand’s North Island, near the South Head of the Kaipara Harbour. It is known for its stunning black sand beach, rugged cliffs, and expansive sand dunes, offering a remote and unspoiled natural environment. The gap is a popular spot for beachgoers, surfers, and off-road enthusiasts seeking an adventurous outing away from more crowded beaches.

Access to Mahuta Gap typically requires a 4WD vehicle, as the route involves navigating through sandy tracks and possibly crossing tidal flats. The 4WD track to Mahuta Gap provides a thrilling experience, with challenging terrain that includes soft sand, steep inclines, and occasionally muddy patches. This makes it suitable for experienced drivers who are equipped to handle off-road conditions.

The area is ideal for those looking to enjoy outdoor activities like fishing, surfing, or simply exploring the wild, untamed coastline. Visitors to Mahuta Gap should be prepared for the remote location, as there are limited facilities and changing weather conditions can impact the accessibility of the tracks. As with all remote coastal areas, it’s important to respect the environment and ensure that vehicles are well-prepared for the journey.
